Why It Matters

The Best Content Still Needs A Technical Foundation

Most of the audits I run start the same way: a business with genuinely good content, wondering why it isn't ranking. The answer is almost never the writing — it's crawl errors, bloated JavaScript, missing schema, or a site structure that buries the pages that matter three clicks deep.

Technical SEO isn't a one-time checklist. It's the layer that determines whether everything else you invest in — content, PR, backlinks — actually gets a fair evaluation from search engines in the first place.

FAQ

Technical SEO Questions

What does technical SEO actually include?+
Crawl and indexation, Core Web Vitals, schema markup, site architecture, XML sitemaps, canonicalization, mobile and security setup, and international SEO where relevant — the foundational layer that lets search engines properly access and understand a site.
How long does a technical SEO fix take to show results?+
Crawl and indexation fixes are often reflected in Search Console within 2-4 weeks. Ranking impact typically compounds over 3-6 months as Google re-crawls and re-evaluates the site.
Do I need technical SEO if my content is already good?+
Yes. Strong content on a site Google can't crawl, index or render properly rarely ranks to its potential — technical SEO is what lets your content actually get evaluated fairly.
Will fixing Core Web Vitals guarantee higher rankings?+
Core Web Vitals are one of many ranking signals, acting more as a tie-breaker than a dominant factor — but they also directly improve conversion rate and user experience, which makes them worth fixing regardless. Read the full Core Web Vitals guide →
Do you provide a report after a technical SEO audit?+
Yes — every audit is delivered as a prioritized, plain-English report ranking issues by impact and effort, not a raw export of crawler data.
